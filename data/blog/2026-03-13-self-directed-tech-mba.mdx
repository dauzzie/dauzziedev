Self-Directed Technology MBA (with Course Links)

This roadmap follows the structure used in the Class Central “Free Online MBA” curriculum:
	1.	Foundations
	2.	Strategy
	3.	Technology & Data
	4.	Entrepreneurship
	5.	Leadership

Source article:
Full Free MBA Curriculum (Class Central)￼

⸻

Stage 1 — Business Foundations

Goal: Learn how businesses operate.

These four courses are the core Wharton MBA fundamentals recommended in the MOOC MBA curriculum.  ￼

1. Introduction to Marketing

https://www.coursera.org/learn/wharton-marketing

2. Introduction to Financial Accounting

https://www.coursera.org/learn/wharton-accounting

3. Introduction to Corporate Finance

https://www.coursera.org/learn/wharton-finance

4. Introduction to Operations Management

https://www.coursera.org/learn/wharton-operations

5. CS50 for Business Professionals (Harvard)

https://www.edx.org/course/cs50s-computer-science-for-business-professionals

Why this stage matters:

You learn how companies measure:
	•	revenue
	•	profit
	•	cost structure
	•	customer demand

Without this, product ideas rarely succeed.

⸻

Stage 2 — Strategy & Competitive Thinking

Goal: Understand how companies win markets.

6. Business Strategy (Wharton)

https://www.coursera.org/learn/wharton-business-strategy

7. Connected Strategy (Harvard Business School)

https://online.hbs.edu/courses/connected-strategy/

8. Decision Making & Scenarios

https://www.coursera.org/learn/wharton-decision-making-scenarios

9. Leadership Through Design Innovation (Northwestern)

https://www.coursera.org/learn/leadership-design-innovation

What you gain:
	•	competitive strategy
	•	innovation frameworks
	•	product positioning

This is where you start thinking like a product leader instead of just an engineer.

⸻

Stage 3 — Technology & Data Strategy

Goal: Understand how technology drives business.

10. Predictive Analytics

https://www.edx.org/course/predictive-analytics

11. Data-Driven Decision Making

https://www.pwc.com/gx/en/services/academy/digital-learning/data-driven-decision-making.html

12. Digital Transformation

https://www.coursera.org/learn/bcg-uva-darden-digital-transformation

What you gain:
	•	analytics strategy
	•	product metrics
	•	digital business models

This is the bridge between engineering and executive decision-making.

⸻

Stage 4 — Entrepreneurship & Product Creation

Goal: Learn how technology companies are built.

13. How to Start a Startup (Stanford)

https://startupclass.samaltman.com/

14. Becoming an Entrepreneur (MIT)

https://www.edx.org/course/becoming-an-entrepreneur

15. Entrepreneurship in Emerging Economies (Harvard)

https://www.edx.org/course/entrepreneurship-in-emerging-economies

16. Product & Service Creation in the Internet Age (MIT Sloan)

https://www.edx.org/course/product-design-the-delta-method

What you gain:
	•	startup validation
	•	product development
	•	business models
	•	growth strategies

⸻

Stage 5 — Leadership & Organizational Design

Goal: Learn how to lead teams and organizations.

17. Managing Social and Human Capital (Wharton)

https://www.coursera.org/learn/wharton-social-human-capital

18. Leading People and Teams (University of Michigan)

https://www.coursera.org/specializations/leading-teams

19. Leadership Communication for Maximum Impact (Northwestern)

https://www.coursera.org/learn/leadership-communication

What you gain:
	•	hiring strategy
	•	team leadership
	•	communication skills

⸻

Capstone Projects (Recommended)

Apply the knowledge while studying.

Project 1 — Build a Startup

Examples:
	•	finance app
	•	AI product
	•	developer tool

Use the courses to guide:
	•	product strategy
	•	pricing model
	•	go-to-market plan

⸻

Project 2 — Analyze a Tech Company

Pick one company:
	•	Apple
	•	Nvidia
	•	Stripe
	•	Airbnb

Break down:
	•	business model
	•	competitive advantage
	•	growth strategy

A Technology MBA teaches the ability to:
	•	identify opportunities
	•	build valuable technology
	•	create profitable businesses
	•	lead organizations

Most engineers know how to build things.

This roadmap teaches how to build things that matter economically.
